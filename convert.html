FAQ - Video to Audio Converter

How do I use this converter step by step?

1) Upload a file or paste a video link. 2) Choose output format. 3) Optionally set Start and End time. 4) Click Convert to Audio. 5) Preview before export. 6) Download Audio or Download Bass Boosted.

What features are included in this tool?

Features include drag-and-drop upload, video-link import, MP3/WAV/AAC/FLAC/OGG output, optional trim by time range, live audio preview, playback controls, volume and bass boost panel, normal download, and bass-boosted download.

What audio formats are supported?

You can convert to MP3 (default), WAV, AAC (M4A), FLAC, and OGG. Extension changes automatically based on selected format.

How does trimming work?

Open Set Start/End Time (Optional) and enter time. Under 60 minutes it uses MM:SS. At 1 hour or more it switches to HH:MM:SS automatically. Examples: 2 means 02:00, 2:2 means 02:20, 5:2:3 means 05:20:30, 50203 means 05:02:03, and 004010 means 00:40:10. If both are 00:00 or 00:00:00, full duration is used.

How does mobile number typing work for time?

In mobile, compact typing is supported. Example: 0022 becomes 00:22, 1256 becomes 12:56, and 2 becomes 02:00 automatically.

How do preview, volume, and bass boost work?

After conversion, use Play to preview trimmed audio. Click Vol to show or hide both sliders. Volume controls playback loudness. Bass Boost increases low frequencies and is also used by Download Bass Boosted.

What is Download Bass Boosted?

This creates a new exported audio file using your current Bass Boost slider setting. The boosted export is downloaded as WAV for stable processing quality.

Are keyboard shortcuts supported in preview?

Yes. Space plays or pauses, M toggles mute, Arrow Up increases volume, and Arrow Down decreases volume.

What are limits and common errors?

Recommended upload size is under 2 GB. Very large files can fail on low-memory devices. If a link fails, upload the file directly or retry with another network.

Useful tips for best output quality

Use MP3 for compatibility, AAC for smaller high-quality files, and FLAC/WAV for editing. Keep trim start and end close to needed audio to reduce file size. Use headphones while previewing and keep Bass Boost under 85% for cleaner sound.

Is this tool free?

Yes. The converter is free to use.
